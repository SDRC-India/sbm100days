Sanitation In Odisha

Odisha started out with an Rural Sanitation Coverage of 11% on 2nd October 2014. As on date Odisha has achieved over 72% Sanitation coverage. Five districts (Debagarh, Jharsuguda, Baleswar, Sambalpur and Kandhamal) have achieved 100% Sanitation Coverage. Over 1500 GPs and more than 17,000 villages across the state have been declared ODF. Since April, 2017 more than 2 million individual household latrines (IHHLs) have been constructed in the State.

SOSO

The Hon’ble CM of Odisha believes that sanitation is one of the most important issues we need to deal as a society. “Swachha Odisha Sustha Odisha” is a concerted effort by Govt. of Odisha to eliminate open defecation, promote the concepts of “Waste to Wealth” and “Reduce, Recycle, Reuse”, and improve the living standards of the people across Odisha. The focus is to provide the people with best services in the sector of drinking water, nutrition, sanitation and public health. Swachha Odisha Sustha Odisha is an initiative which envisages a future filled with hope and prosperity for all Odias.

SBM(G)

SBM (G) aims to achieve a clean and ODF India by 2nd October, 2019. It seeks to bring about an improvement in the general quality of life in the rural areas, by promoting cleanliness, hygiene and eliminating open defecation. It pursues community involvement in order to adopt sustainable sanitation practices and facilities through awareness creation and health education. It encourages cost effective and appropriate technologies for ecologically safe and sustainable sanitation. SBM(G) is designed to create significant positive impact on gender and promote social inclusion by improving sanitation especially in marginalized communities.
